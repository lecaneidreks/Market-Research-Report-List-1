<p><p>The lower limb exoskeleton market encompasses two primary types: powered and passive exoskeletons. Powered exoskeletons utilize motors and sensors to assist wearers with movement, often enhancing mobility for individuals with disabilities or aiding in rehabilitation. In contrast, passive exoskeletons rely on mechanical structures and springs to support users without the need for external power. They primarily reduce fatigue during activities like lifting or walking, making them beneficial for labor-intensive occupations. Both types serve distinct needs in mobility and rehabilitation.</p></p>
