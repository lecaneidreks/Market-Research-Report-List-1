<p><p>The organophosphorus-based flame retardants (FRs) market is divided into two primary types: halogen-containing and halogen-free. Halogen-containing flame retardants incorporate chlorine or bromine, offering effective fire resistance but raising environmental and health concerns due to emissions during combustion. In contrast, halogen-free flame retardants rely on phosphorus-based compounds, providing safer alternatives with lower toxicity and environmental impact. The growing demand for sustainable solutions is driving the shift towards halogen-free options in various applications, including textiles, plastics, and electronics.</p></p>
