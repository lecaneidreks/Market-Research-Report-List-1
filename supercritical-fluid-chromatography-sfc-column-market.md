<p><p>Supercritical Fluid Chromatography (SFC) columns are categorized into two main types: chiral and non-chiral. Chiral SFC columns are designed for separating enantiomers, crucial in pharmaceuticals and fine chemicals, ensuring the purity and efficacy of chiral compounds. Non-chiral SFC columns, on the other hand, are used for separating achiral compounds or mixtures, making them suitable for a broader range of applications, including environmental analysis and food safety. Both types play vital roles in analytical and preparative chemistry.</p></p>
