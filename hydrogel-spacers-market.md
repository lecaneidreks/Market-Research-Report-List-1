<p><p>The hydrogel spacers market is primarily categorized into two types based on their composition: Polyethylene Glycol (PEG) base and Hyaluronic Acid (HA) base. PEG-based hydrogels are known for their biocompatibility and adaptability, making them suitable for various medical applications, including cancer therapy. In contrast, HA-based hydrogels leverage the natural properties of hyaluronic acid, providing excellent moisture retention and promoting tissue healing. Both types are used to create physical barriers in treatments, enhancing procedural outcomes and patient comfort.</p></p>
